CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television) in home automation refers to the integration of surveillance cameras with automated systems to enhance security and monitoring capabilities within residential spaces. These systems typically include strategically placed cameras that transmit video footage to a centralized monitoring station or to connected devices such as smartphones and tablets. In residential applications, CCTV home automation provides homeowners with real-time visibility and control over their property's security, regardless of their physical location. This enables them to monitor activities around their home, deter potential intruders, and promptly respond to security alerts or incidents. Modern CCTV systems often feature high-definition cameras with night vision capabilities, ensuring clear footage even in low-light conditions. Integration with home automation platforms allows CCTV systems to collaborate with other smart devices such as motion sensors, smart locks, and lighting systems. For instance, motion detection by CCTV cameras can trigger lights to turn on or alert homeowners via their mobile devices. Some systems also offer advanced features like facial recognition technology or the ability to store footage in the cloud for easy access and review.
